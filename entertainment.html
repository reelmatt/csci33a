Movies & TV

My Experience

From a very young age, one of my favorite things to do was not just watch movies and television, but to specifically watch all of the bonus features and behind-the-scenes videos that came with the movie. Watching people make movies was just so fascinating to me and I became determined to make films of my own.

Starting with an English project freshman year of high school, I got more deeply involved in all things video production. From countless high school projects, I applied to film school and attended Emerson College. Four years later, and even more videos under my belt, I graduated with my undergraduate degree in Film Editing, and now use that in my day job editing educational videos.

Favorite entertainment

The way I see it, in order to be a good filmmaker you need to enjoy movies. I'll watch pretty much any genre of movie or television, and try to appreciate what each is trying to accomplish. Taking inspiration from Honky's Movie Year blog, which I heard of on a podcast, I ventured on a movie year of my own starting in 2012. While I haven't kept up as well as Honky has, I did succeed in watching 365 films in 365 days my first year. Some movies were great, some were bad, all I rated and reviewed on my own blog.

To get a brief sense of some of my favorite movies and television shows, check out the lists for a semi-random assortment. To check-out a list of all my five-star rated films, you can see that on Letterboxd (unfortunately, I don't have a similar service for tracking TV shows).
