Beer

Tastings

No one likes beer when they first try it. I was the same way when I turned 21. Even if you like some beers, you may have a hard time liking every style. My palette isn't very sophisticated — trying to pick out all the subtle flavors and intricacies isn't why I like drinking beer — but it has expanded from light beers to a wide variety of IPAs, hefeweizens, red and brown ales, and many more. Often when I try a new style I will think, "Eh, this doesn't really taste that great", before I finally think, "Ooo, this is actually quite good."

Whenever I travel, one of my favorite things to do is visit a local brewery, see what's on tap, and try a flight of beers. Two destinations that had a smattering of great breweries were Portland, Maine, and Denver, Colorado. Here's just a sample of some of the breweries I visited on my trips there.

Homebrewing

Since 2015, shortly after graduating college and having some free time on my hands, I decided to get into homebrewing. With the boom in craft- and micro-breweries, homebrewing has also been growing. After doing a lot of reserach, mainly reading the bible of homebrewing, How to Brew by John Palmer, I swung by my local homebrew store, picked up the necessary equipment and ingredients, and got to work.

Now, four years and twelve beers later, I've learned a lot, made some mistakes, as well as some truly tasty beers. The frequency with which I brew has decreased from when I started, but I try to make a habit of making at least one new beer a year.
